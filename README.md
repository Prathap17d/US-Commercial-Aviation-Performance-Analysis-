# US Commercial Aviation Operational Performance Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes operational performance across the United States commercial aviation network using the 2015 Bureau of Transportation Statistics (BTS) On-Time Reporting dataset containing approximately 5.82 million flight records.

The objective is to identify operational bottlenecks, delay patterns, cancellation drivers, airport performance issues, and provide data-driven recommendations to improve airline reliability and passenger experience.

---

## Business Problem

Airlines experience delays, cancellations, and operational inefficiencies that negatively impact customer satisfaction and operational costs.

This project aims to:

- Measure On-Time Performance (OTP)
- Analyze delay patterns
- Identify cancellation drivers
- Evaluate airport and carrier performance
- Provide strategic recommendations

---

## Dataset

Source: Bureau of Transportation Statistics (BTS)

- 5.82 Million Flight Records
- 14 Commercial Airlines
- Domestic Flights (2015)

## Key Findings

### Network Performance

- OTP Rate: 80.99%
- Cancellation Rate: 1.54%
- Average Departure Delay: 9.23 Minutes
- Average Arrival Delay: 4.3 Minutes

### Operational Insights

- June had the highest delays.
- October was the best-performing month.
- Delays peaked around 7 PM.
- IFP Airport recorded the highest departure delays.
- ITH Airport recorded the highest cancellation rate.

---

## Strategic Recommendations

1. Introduce scheduling buffers during late afternoon operations.
2. Improve infrastructure at high-delay regional airports.
3. Increase seasonal operational readiness during summer and winter periods.
